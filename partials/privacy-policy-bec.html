2. Purpose of This Policy

This policy explains how we collect, use, store, and share your information when you use our Business Energy Claim Checker, sign a Letter of Authority (LOA), upload documents, or otherwise contact us about a business energy claim. This service is not FCA-regulated.

3. The Data We Collect

We may collect:

  • Business details (business name, type, registration number)
  • Contact details (contact name, email, phone)
  • Energy contract details (supplier, contract dates, contract length, contract status)
  • Business address and trading address
  • Description of the issue or mis-selling concern
  • Supporting documents you upload (e.g., energy contracts, bills, correspondence)
  • LOA status and signed LOA PDF
  • Marketing source and consent preferences
  • Interaction logs (e.g., contact events, case status updates)
  • Technical data from essential cookies; analytics cookies only with consent (see Cookies)

4. How We Collect Your Data

Directly from you (our forms, email, SMS/WhatsApp, phone). We may also receive updates from specialist commercial solicitors handling your claim and from your energy supplier where you have authorised disclosure.

5. How We Use Your Data

  • Assess your potential business energy mis-selling claim
  • Generate and send your LOA for e-signature where required
  • Provide you a secure link to upload supporting documents (optional)
  • Share your information with a specialist commercial solicitor to review and progress your claim
  • Communicate with you about your case via email and, where applicable, SMS/WhatsApp
  • Maintain internal audit and case-management logs
  • Improve our services and website (analytics only with consent)

Automated steps: certain steps (e.g., sending the LOA, issuing document-upload links, logging status changes, and routing data to our systems) are automated. We do not carry out automated decision-making that produces legal or similarly significant effects.

Controller roles: We act as the controller for our processing activities. Once your data is shared with a solicitor, that firm acts as an independent controller for its legal services.

6. Lawful Basis

Our primary basis is consent (UK GDPR Art. 6(1)(a)) for claim assessment, contacting you, and sharing information with commercial solicitors. We may also rely on:

  • Legitimate interests (Art. 6(1)(f)) for commercial claims assessment, fraud prevention, security, service improvement, and maintaining audit logs (balanced against your rights)
  • Contract (Art. 6(1)(b)) where we enter into an agreement with you for claims services

Note: We do NOT rely on FCA regulatory obligations as a lawful basis for this service because business energy claims are unregulated.

You can withdraw consent at any time by emailing support@visiblelegal.co.uk. Withdrawal does not affect prior processing.

  • Specialist commercial solicitors: we share your case details with solicitors who specialise in business energy mis-selling to review and progress your claim.
  • Service providers (processors): we use reputable vendors for secure processing, including:
    • Airtable (case records and interaction logs)
    • eSignatures.io (LOA e-signature and signed LOA PDFs)
    • Dropbox (secure file requests and document storage)
    • Make (automation/orchestration between systems)
    • Email/SMS/WhatsApp providers as needed to contact you
    These providers act under our instructions and we have appropriate data processing agreements in place.
  • Regulators or authorities: where required by law (e.g., ICO).

We do not sell your personal or business data.

7A. Data Subjects

Our business energy claims service is intended for businesses and their authorised representatives. Where individual personal data is provided (e.g., a business contact's name and email), it is protected under UK GDPR in the same way as any other personal data.

8. Retention

  • Form-only enquiries without LOA or continued consent: retained for up to 90 days for follow-up and then deleted or anonymised.
  • Active claims (with LOA and solicitor referral): retained for up to 6 years after closure for record-keeping and potential dispute resolution.

We retain cookie consent records for up to 12 months. We review our retention periods regularly and may retain data longer where required by law or to establish, exercise, or defend legal claims.
