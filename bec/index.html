What is a business energy claim?

Secret commissions between brokers and suppliers

A business energy claim is based on hidden commissions paid by energy suppliers to energy brokers without the customer's knowledge. Under English law, these undisclosed payments are treated as bribes — making the arrangement between broker and supplier unfair and unlawful.

You trusted a broker to find the best deal

Your business used an energy broker to source the most suitable and competitive energy contract. The broker was expected to act in your best interests.

Hidden commissions weren't disclosed

Instead of finding you the best deal, the broker selected contracts that paid them the highest level of secret commission from the energy supplier — without telling you.

These payments are treated as bribes

Under English law, secret commissions paid between a broker and supplier without the customer's knowledge are treated as bribes. This makes the arrangement unfair and unlawful.

You may be entitled to recover losses

If your broker received undisclosed commissions, you have the right to identify and recover the losses caused by inflated energy costs that you unknowingly paid.
